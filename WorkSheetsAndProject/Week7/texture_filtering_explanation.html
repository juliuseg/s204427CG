Texture Filtering Modes

Texture filtering controls how textures look when resized. Here’s a quick rundown of the main filtering options:

1. Nearest

Uses the closest texel color without smoothing. This can look blocky or pixelated when zoomed in.

2. Linear

Blends colors of nearby texels to smooth the texture. Reduces blockiness but can look blurry when scaled down.

3. Nearest Mipmap Nearest

Chooses the nearest mipmap level (precomputed smaller textures) and applies nearest filtering within that level. Reduces aliasing but may cause “popping” when switching between levels.

4. Linear Mipmap Nearest

Chooses the nearest mipmap level and applies linear filtering within that level. Smooths texture a bit more but still has visible transitions between mipmaps.

5. Nearest Mipmap Linear

Interpolates between two mipmap levels with nearest filtering. Reduces popping but may still look pixelated within levels.

6. Linear Mipmap Linear

The smoothest option, blending both between mipmap levels and within each level. Ideal for high quality but slower to render.

Summary

Nearest: Fast, but blocky.
Linear: Smoother, can be blurry.
Mipmaps: Helps reduce aliasing, with various levels of smoothness and performance.
