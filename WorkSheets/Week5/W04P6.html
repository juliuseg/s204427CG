Computer Graphics Questions

(a) Difference between Phong Shading and Phong Lighting

Phong Reflection Model (or Phong lighting): A method for calculating light interaction with surfaces, using ambient, diffuse, and specular components.

Phong Shading: Applies the Phong reflection model per pixel, resulting in smoother highlights.

Summary: Phong lighting describes the light reflection model, while Phong shading describes applying this model per fragment for smoother visuals.

(b) Difference between Flat, Gouraud, and Phong Shading

  • Flat Shading: Lighting is computed once per face, giving a uniform color.
    Pros: Fast, suitable for faceted surfaces.
    Cons: Unrealistic on curved surfaces.
  • Gouraud Shading: Lighting is calculated per vertex, with colors interpolated across the surface.
    Pros: More realistic than flat shading; efficient.
    Cons: Inaccurate highlights, especially on large polygons.
  • Phong Shading: Lighting is computed per pixel for smooth gradients.
    Pros: High realism, especially for specular highlights.
    Cons: More computationally expensive.

Best for Highlights: Phong shading captures highlights more accurately.

(c) Difference between Directional and Point Lights

Directional Light: Represents light from a distant source (e.g., the sun), with parallel rays.

Point Light: Represents light emitted from a point, with intensity decreasing by distance.

(d) Influence of Eye Position on Shading

The eye position affects the specular highlight by influencing the view direction, which determines the highlight’s intensity and placement.

(e) Effect of Setting Specular Term to (0,0,0)

Setting the specular term to (0,0,0) removes the specular component, making the object appear matte with no reflective highlights.

(f) Effect of Increasing the Shininess Exponent (α)

Higher shininess exponent values make specular highlights sharper and smaller, simulating smooth surfaces like metal.
